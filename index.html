Why does this matter?

Foot health is critical to the overall well-being of seniors, directly impacting mobility, independence, and quality of life. Our AI technology empowers healthcare providers with actionable insights, enabling them to offer timely, personalized care.

1. Foot Health and Fall Risk: Corns, Calluses, and Foot Pain.
  • Elderly individuals with foot pain were nearly 2.5 times more likely to experience falls than those without pain.
  • Falls are the leading cause of injury-related hospital admissions for older adults. According to the CDC, falls cause over 3 million emergency department visits annually for older adults, many of which are linked to preventable foot health issues.
  • A study in the British Journal of Pain concluded that people with foot pain or conditions like corns were two to four times more likely to experience a fall. These minor deformities can also lead to the development of more serious complications, including infections.
2. Fungal Infections and Infections in General:
  • Fungal infections (such as nail fungus) are prevalent in older adults, affecting about 10-15% of the population over age 60. If left untreated, these infections can lead to severe dermatological issues and secondary infections (such as cellulitis), which may require hospitalization.
3. Hospital Admissions:
  • In seniors, foot infections (including fungal, bacterial, or diabetic foot ulcers) are a leading cause of hospitalization, especially in those with comorbidities such as diabetes or vascular disease. According to research in Clinical Infectious Diseases, about 40-60% of older adults with diabetic foot ulcers require hospitalization due to complications from infections.
  • Hospitalization rates for foot-related issues are also elevated in those with neglected foot care, and early interventions such as regular inspections can reduce the need for emergency care. For instance, in elderly patients with diabetes, a study in The Journal of Diabetes and its Complications found that routine foot examinations can reduce hospitalization rates by 40-50% by catching issues before they progress to severe infections or amputations.
4. Prevention and Health Costs:
  • Timely detection and intervention can significantly reduce the costs associated with long-term care. For example, early treatment of fungal infections and other foot conditions can lower the need for more expensive treatments like surgeries or hospital stays, reducing the financial burden on healthcare systems.
  • According to the National Institutes of Health, foot problems in seniors can result in increased health care costs due to frequent doctor visits, treatments, medications, and hospital stays for complications from untreated conditions. Early interventions can reduce the cost burden by up to 40%.
5. Impact of Regular Foot Care:
  • In clinical practice, regular foot care—including routine assessments for corns, calluses, fungal infections, and diabetic ulcers—can significantly prevent complications. A review published in the Journal of Aging Research found that seniors who undergo regular foot care assessments have a 30-50% lower risk of falls, injuries, and hospital admissions related to foot complications compared to those who do not have such assessments.
